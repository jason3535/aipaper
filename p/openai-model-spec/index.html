核心贡献 · Key contributions

  • 提出模型规范，一个通过目标、规则和默认行为来塑造期望模型行为的框架。
    Introduces the Model Spec, a framework for shaping desired model behavior with objectives, rules, and defaults.
  • 明确三个目标：协助开发者和最终用户、造福人类、良好体现OpenAI形象。
    Specifies three objectives: assist developer and end user, benefit humanity, and reflect well on OpenAI.
  • 包含安全和合法性规则，如遵守法律和避免不宜工作内容。
    Includes rules for safety and legality, such as complying with laws and avoiding NSFW content.
  • 提供处理冲突的默认行为，如假设善意和提出澄清性问题。
    Provides default behaviors for handling conflicts, like assuming best intentions and asking clarifying questions.
  • 将权限委托给开发者和最终用户，开发者消息优先于用户指令。
    Delegates authority to developers and end users, with developer messages taking precedence over user instructions.
  • 旨在提供信息而非影响，呈现事实同时尊重用户信念。
    Aims to inform rather than influence, presenting facts while respecting user beliefs.

局限 · Limitations

  • 模型规范为草案且预期会变化，缺乏对其有效性的实证验证。
    The Model Spec is a draft and expected to change, lacking empirical validation of its effectiveness.
  • 依赖人类反馈和基于人类反馈的强化学习（RLHF），可能引入偏见和可扩展性问题。
    Relies on human feedback and RLHF, which may introduce biases and scalability issues.
  • 如“不要试图改变任何人想法”的规则可能与事实性在错误信息案例中冲突。
    Rules like 'don't try to change anyone's mind' may conflict with factuality in misinformation cases.
  • 法律合规在不同司法管辖区复杂，规范未解决管辖冲突。
    Legal compliance is complex across jurisdictions, and the spec does not resolve jurisdictional conflicts.
  • 默认行为可能无法覆盖所有边缘情况，导致模型响应不一致。
    Default behaviors may not cover all edge cases, leading to inconsistent model responses.
