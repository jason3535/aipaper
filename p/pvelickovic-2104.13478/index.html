Geometric Deep Learning: Grids, Groups, Graphs, Geodesics, and Gauges

摘要 · Abstract

过去十年见证了数据科学和机器学习的实验革命，以深度学习方法为代表。事实上，许多以前被认为遥不可及的高维学习任务——例如计算机视觉、围棋或蛋白质折叠——在适当的计算规模下是可行的。值得注意的是，深度学习的本质建立在两个简单的算法原理之上：第一，表示或特征学习的概念，通过适应性的、通常是层次化的特征来捕捉每个任务的适当规律；第二，通过局部梯度下降类型的方法进行学习，通常以反向传播实现。虽然在高维中学习通用函数是一个诅咒性的估计问题，但大多数感兴趣的任务并非通用，而是带有来自物理世界底层低维性和结构的基本预定义规律。本文旨在通过统一的几何原理揭示这些规律，这些原理可广泛应用于各种领域。这种“几何统一”的努力，秉承菲利克斯·克莱因的埃尔朗根纲领精神，具有双重目的：一方面，它提供了一个共同的数学框架来研究最成功的神经网络架构，如CNN、RNN、GNN和Transformer；另一方面，它提供了一种建设性的程序，将先验物理知识融入神经架构，并为构建尚未发明的未来架构提供原则性方法。

The last decade has witnessed an experimental revolution in data science and machine learning, epitomised by deep learning methods. Indeed, many high-dimensional learning tasks previously thought to be beyond reach -- such as computer vision, playing Go, or protein folding -- are in fact feasible with appropriate computational scale. Remarkably, the essence of deep learning is built from two simple algorithmic principles: first, the notion of representation or feature learning, whereby adapted, often hierarchical, features capture the appropriate notion of regularity for each task, and second, learning by local gradient-descent type methods, typically implemented as backpropagation. While learning generic functions in high dimensions is a cursed estimation problem, most tasks of interest are not generic, and come with essential pre-defined regularities arising from the underlying low-dimensionality and structure of the physical world. This text is concerned with exposing these regularities through unified geometric principles that can be applied throughout a wide spectrum of applications. Such a 'geometric unification' endeavour, in the spirit of Felix Klein's Erlangen Program, serves a dual purpose: on one hand, it provides a common mathematical framework to study the most successful neural network architectures, such as CNNs, RNNs, GNNs, and Transformers. On the other hand, it gives a constructive procedure to incorporate prior physical knowledge into neural architectures and provide principled way to build future architectures yet to be invented.

核心贡献 · Key contributions

  • 将深度学习架构（CNN、RNN、GNN、Transformer）统一在几何原理之下。
    Unifies deep learning architectures (CNNs, RNNs, GNNs, Transformers) under geometric principles.
  • 揭示高维学习任务利用低维结构和物理规律。
    Reveals that high-dimensional learning tasks leverage low-dimensional structure and physical regularities.
  • 提供将先验物理知识融入神经架构的构造性方法。
    Provides a constructive procedure to incorporate prior physical knowledge into neural architectures.
  • 将Felix Klein的Erlangen纲领扩展到现代机器学习，实现有原则的架构设计。
    Extends Felix Klein's Erlangen Program to modern machine learning for principled architecture design.
  • 证明深度学习的成功源于表示学习和局部梯度下降。
    Demonstrates that deep learning's success stems from representation learning and local gradient descent.
  • 为研究和发明神经网络架构提供统一的数学框架。
    Offers a common mathematical framework for studying and inventing neural network architectures.

局限 · Limitations

  • 聚焦几何原理，可能未涵盖优化或泛化等所有深度学习方面。
    Focuses on geometric principles, may not cover all deep learning aspects like optimization or generalization.
  • 假设存在底层低维结构，但并非所有实际任务都满足。
    Assumes underlying low-dimensional structure, which may not hold for all real-world tasks.
  • 主要为理论性，未涉及实际实现细节和计算成本。
    Primarily theoretical; practical implementation details and computational costs are not addressed.
  • 统一可能过度简化CNN与Transformer等架构间的差异。
    The unification may oversimplify differences between architectures like CNNs and Transformers.
  • 未对所提几何框架提供实证验证或基准测试。
    Does not provide empirical validation or benchmarks for the proposed geometric framework.
